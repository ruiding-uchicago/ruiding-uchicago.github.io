== RESEARCH PHILOSOPHY — the "no-database regime" ==
Rui builds AI co-scientist systems for complex materials and device discovery in the no-database regime: problems with high system complexity, high data cost, and no existing database or benchmark. Complex functional materials and devices (FET sensors, membranes, electrocatalytic assemblies, fuel cell and electrolyzer components) are multi-component and strongly coupled across scales, and one data point can cost days of synthesis and testing. Instead of waiting for big data that will not arrive, he designs AI that works like an experimentalist: pull priors from the literature, build physics-constrained surrogates, and spend the experimental budget where it counts.

== THE AI CO-SCIENTIST STACK (four pillars) ==
The stack runs: hypothesis generation -> device digital twins -> rapid atomistic validation -> wet-lab experiment -> feedback, under the umbrella project BRAINIAC.

1) DToR (Deep Tree of Research) — hypothesis generation.
A local-first deep-research agent with a tree-structured orchestrator that adaptively expands and prunes research branches. Across 27 nanomaterials/device topics in a 44-agent benchmark, its reports reached a ~79% mean pairwise win rate against commercial deep-research systems, running entirely on in-house consumer-level hardware with open-source LLMs. Paper: https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.18303 . Presented at NeurIPS 2025 AI4Mat workshop.

2) T3 (Text-Twin-Translation) — device digital twins.
Text: an agentic pipeline, prompt-optimized with TextGrad, extracts structured knowledge graphs from raw publication corpora (21.8% BLEU improvement in knowledge extraction). Twin: a graph neural network that knows the device topology, trained as a digital twin of the coupled material-device system; on FET sensors it predicts sensitivity with 92.3% accuracy. Translation: the twin screens 123.2 million PubChem compounds for out-of-distribution tasks such as PFAS-sensing probe design. Accepted at SIGKDD 2026 AI4Science Track (CORE A*); Spotlight Oral at ICLR 2026 AI4Mat workshop. Code: https://github.com/ruiding-uchicago/T3_FET_sensor . OpenReview: https://openreview.net/forum?id=7QfGX651NZ .

3) RAPIDS — rapid physical validation.
The atomistic validation engine. It benchmarks machine-learning interatomic potentials (MLIPs) against DFT on 5,567 probe-target dimer interactions across 18 benchmarks, and finds that geometry, not the energy surface, drives the neutral MLIP-DFT gap. Packaged as a tool autonomous LLM agents can call for fast physical sanity checks. Accepted at ICML 2026 AI4Physics workshop. OpenReview: https://openreview.net/forum?id=r2NSBJ4ip7 .

4) Experiments — the wet lab closes the loop: nanomaterial synthesis, device fabrication, electrochemical testing.
